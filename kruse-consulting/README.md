# Kruse Consulting — Data / Analytics Engineer (Async Video Screener)

**Format:** 4 questions, self-recorded video answers (phone camera is fine). They explicitly asked for *authentic, not polished* — one take with small stumbles lands better than a memorized script.

---

## The 4 questions

1. **What specifically interests you about this opportunity?**
2. **What accomplishment are you most proud of, and why?**
3. **Tell us about a time you faced unclear requirements — how did you create structure and keep stakeholders aligned?**
4. **What's a recent industry trend that excites you, and why?**

---

## What works (learned by doing it)

- **Q2 wants one specific, quantifiable story** — a real system, real numbers (runtime cut, cost saved, SLA record), three concrete technical decisions. Not a career montage.
- **Q3 is a process question in disguise.** The strong shape: the mistake you used to make → the reframe (walk the stakeholder through a real scenario end-to-end, then mirror back your understanding as a diagram) → proof you now reuse that pattern.
- **Q4 is a depth check.** Pick a trend you have actually touched, and land one non-obvious insight about it — "AI is exciting" is a zero.
- Recording mechanics matter more than you'd think: light in front of you, plain background, look at the lens for the open and close of each answer.
